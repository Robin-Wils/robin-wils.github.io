The ABCs of Death franchise

The ABCs of Death Franchise are movies which contain different short films from many different directors. It is a nice watch to find directors with a style which you like.

I wouldn’t call these movies very good, but they can be worth watching.

The ABCs of Death

It was overall not that impressive, but there were some nice shorts and it was neat to see, just to find which directors you like.

I didn’t expect that I liked the style of the short of the guy who directed A Serbian Film that much. Maybe I should watch it some day.

The short of Yûdai Yamaguchi was good fun. I will probably check more of his work, since I haven’t heard of him. I like practical effects, like the ones he used in his short.

Xavier Gens also had a great one. It was just a good idea to do in a short especially if you are already know for Frontière(s), a New French Extremity movie (same genre as Martyrs).

The reason why I haven’t watched that movie yet, is because I think that it is too similar to Texas Chainsaw Massacre. I might check it out some day, but not immediately.

Yoshihiro Nishimura showed how weird his style was in “Z”. He directed Tokyo Gore Police, which was weird too. His short was just a bit too much for me, but I respect it. It isn’t easy to make.

“F”, from a director which he has worked with also was weird. It was just weird. I didn’t like it. It also didn’t look hard to make, and was just too ridiculous. It does not surprise me that they have worked together before. They even did that with Tokyo Gore Police.

There were more great shorts, but there were some bad ones too. I didn’t like the first ones.

I did like “L”, because you don’t seem to get the full story in that one. It is weird, but also original. I liked the stop motion stuff too. I don’t love the found footage film style though. It can work, but I usually don’t like it.

The ABCs of Death 2

The overall quality of shots, seemed better in this one compared to the first. I did however, like the first one better, some of the stories of the shorts just were better in the first one in my opinion.

I liked “D”. Stop motion is usually neat. I did like the style of this particular stop motion short though.

“P” was a bit different, but fun and interesting. It was a black and white short.

“U” was interesting and brought a bit of a message. There is no such thing as Utopia. Cool concept for a short. Nothing too special though.

“W” was fun. It had 90s commercial style.

“Y” was a mess, of weird stuff, but it was at least an interesting mess. I liked it.

“Z” was interesting. A woman lets a child grow big inside her. Pretty nice effects. Cool idea.
