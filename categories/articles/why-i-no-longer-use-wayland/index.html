Why Wayland?

Wayland is a display server. It is more secure and less complex than Xorg. There are also some other benefits. Wayland is the future.

You can read the following article for some Wayland tips: Trying Wayland and Sway

Why not Wayland?

Drivers

Some drivers seem to rely on Xorg, like some drawing tablet drivers. Some drawing tablet drivers already work though.

The closed source NVIDIA drivers are also not supported yet, but honestly use AMD instead if you have the option.

There are many reasons to not like NVIDIA for GNU/Linux users, but that article is for another day.

Jumpapp

Jumpapp doesn’t works with tablayout in Sway. It does work in i3. I highly recommend using tablayout and jumpapp. It increases my productivity a lot.

Set some jumpapp bindings in your i3 config. Having everything in fullscreen gives you focus. Tiling is a bit overrated in my opinion.

It is useful to have a key to have two windows on your screen, but having too many windows on your screen makes things complex.

Keyboard configuration

Some keyboard configuration isn’t available on Wayland yet. I can’t configure Shift to do Shift lock instead on Wayland.

Swapping CapsLock with Ctrl worked though. One way around this issue is by making your own keyboard layout. I don’t find that user-friendly enough.

Sharing your screen

Sharing your screen isn’t that well-supported on Wayland yet. Recording your screen is possible, but you need to use the supported applications.

My opinion

I do believe that Wayland will get some support and I loved using it. Most things work and I would love to use it when some of these minor problems get fixed.
